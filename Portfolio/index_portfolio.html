CC4. Replication. Re-create, then improve, someone else's chart

Finding a chart that a journalist, think tank, television channel or company has used and to improve it.


Misleading information in the 2017 Chilean presidential elections

In the 2017 presidential debate, the 2 times Chilean president, Sebastián Piñera, presented a graph with which he sought to demonstrate that victimization had decreased during his previous administration. However, the graph sparked controversy due to the misleading scale of the bars. According to the bar representing 22.8% (in blue) appeared to be one-third the size of the bar representing 30.7%, whereas, in reality, one-third of 30.7% is approximately 10.2%. This distortion aimed to exaggerate the differences between the bars.

To address this issue, I have recreated the chart on the right with accurately proportioned bars to reflect the true differences in values.

About COMTRADE data:

For this challenge, I connected to the COMTRADE API to download information of exports of my home country, Chile. The COMTRADE data has very granular information on the value of what each country exports/imports, divided by country of destiny, product, and period (years or months). This is very useful to analyze trade tendencies and evaluate Economic Complexities of countries, something that I will do in my Project.

About the graph:

The graph shows the main destination of Chilean exports. Surprisingly, China represents 40% of all exports, while USA is in the second place, with only 15%. This shows how dependent Chile on China's economy and growth.

CC10. Option 2. Interactive Visualizations

Producing two visualizations that have Interactive elements.


a) Economies' Complexity and Income

In this challenge, I examined the relationship between Economic Complexity and the most common economic aggregate measure to compare income across countries: GDP per capita, adjusted by Purchase Power Parity (PPP). The chart shows a significant correlation, were more complex economies are associated with higher income levels across all years for which data is available.

Additionally, it is noteworthy that there are clear clusters by continent in the X and Y coordinates, a pattern that remains consistent over time. This clustering can be more clearly observed by clicking each continent's name. Feel free to click the continents or move the year selector to interact with the chart.


b) Export matrix of a Complex economy

To illustrate a complex economy, the treemap below shows the top 5 countries with the most complex exports. Top complex countries have diversified export matrices, with products that are relatively more difficult to elaborate, and that fewer countries can produce. Thus, these countries possess a collective know how across various products, allowing them to engage in global trade without relying on a single commodity.
