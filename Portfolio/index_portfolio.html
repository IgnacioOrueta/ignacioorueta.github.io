Misleading information in the 2017 Chilean presidential elections:

In the 2017 presidential debate, the 2 times president of Chile, Sebastián Piñera, presented a graph with which he sought to demonstrate that the victimization figures had decreased during his past government. However, the graph left some doubts relative to the scale of the bars showed. According to the bars on the poster, 22.8% (in blue) looks like one third of 30.7%. In reality, one third of 30.7% is 10.2%.

About COMTRADE data

For this challenge, I connected to the COMTRADE API to download information of exports of my home country, Chile. The COMTRADE data has very granular information on the value of what each country exports/imports, divided by country of destiny, product, and period (years or months). This is very useful to analyze trade tendencies and evaluate Economic Complexities of countries, something that I will do in my Project.

About the graph:

The graph shows that Copper was the main export of Chilean during 2023, representing 29% of the total, followed by Ores, slag and ash (25%). This shows how concentrated the production matrix of this country is, and how dependent it is on the fluctuations of these commodities' prices. For the details on how I elaborate this graph, you can check the Python Script (working on the colors).

CC7. Maps. Base maps and choropleths.

Challenge: producing two maps and embedding them into my portfolio page.

In this exercise, I analyzed Economic Complexity Data from the Harvard Atlas of Economic Complexity. On one hand, the left chart shows how the Complexity of EU economies performed after the formation of EU in 1993 (the first value available was 1995). Then, the right chart shows countries' growth projections for the next decade, based on the Economic Complexity Index (ECI). This identifies those countries whose economic complexity exceeds expectations based on its income level. It is worth mentioning that the ECI has been shown to do better than any other single measure in predicting growth (Atlas, 2022).

CC10. Option 2. Interactive Visualizations.

Challenge: producing two visualizations that have Interactive elements.

In this challenge, I examined the relationship between Economic Complexity and some widely recognized economic aggregate measures of income and inequality, such as GDP per capita (PPP) and the Gini Index. The findings indicate a significant correlation in both cases, showing that a more complex production matrix is associated with higher income levels and lower inequality across all years for which data is available. Additionally, it is noteworthy that there are clear clusters by continent in the X,Y coordinates, a pattern that remains consistent over time. This clustering can be more clearly observed by clicking the name of each continent.
